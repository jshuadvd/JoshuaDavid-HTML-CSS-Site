To Be A Software Craftsman...

Precision, diligence, determination and rigor are terms that come to mind while thinking of a software craftsman. The thing is, I don't see that it ends there, or anywhere for that matter. Being a craftsman means "master of your craft," which is knowing your tools and being a perfectionist. With software you constantly have to improve and learn new technologies. That is the great part! You can keep growing and advancing indefinitely.

Craftsmanship is not a destination; it's a life-long discipline. Not only must you continue to improve, but you have to take pride in your work and strive to be the best that you can. Being a software craftsman is a privilege and an honor.

Why Do I Want To Be A Software Craftsman?

Creating software is partially about breaking down everything to first principals, having a solid foundation, and in developing software, you are able to take a blank slate and create something that will be able to simplify the lives of millions of people. I also hold closely the qualities of what I think it means to be a software craftsman and I strive to attain that goal and personification.

As mentioned previously, I love software and technology in general, as cliche as it may sound, I can do what I love and help others while doing it! .
